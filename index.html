Welcome to Our School

Mission Our Story Objective

At the RoboLectro STEAM Studio, we transpose from idea generation to implementation, tapping into each childâ€™s natural desire to experiment and explore. Our focus is to offer the best possible learning through project-based activities and hand-on experience that grant students an opportunity to solve real-world problems through out-of-the-box thinking and develop the skills that are necessary to excel in the technologically-advanced world.

We started RoboLectro STEAM Studio to follow our passion. The origin of our story starts during the time we were in the USA, where we heard about the LEGO Mindstorm EV3 Robotics kit. Looking at the price of the kit, we were a little hesitant to buy it for our 7 year old son. Since it was very interesting, we bought it and introduced it to our kid. Within a couple of hours, he indulged himself in building the robot and was thoroughly enjoying it. Over the years, he has learnt building and writing the code for the robots and competed in many robotics competitions like WRO, Robofest, etc. We being engineers and working in the corporate world, made us realize how his skills of Design, Mechanical, Electronics, writing software, observation, curiosity and science concepts have grown and how it would be useful for his future. As a parent, I personally felt that if it has helped our kid enhance his overall skills, then robotics and the practical method of learning science would certainly make a difference in the way kids learn science. We took the franchisee of the Science Utsav to teach Practical Science and started RoboLectro STEAM Studio to teach STEM concepts through LEGO robotics.

There is a huge gap in between how Science is taught in developed countries against our Indian education system. STEM(Science, Technology, Engineering and Maths) is never taught within the four walls. Our curriculum has been developed in such a way that the students learn STEM concepts through hands-on, practical and activity based experiences. In order to learn STEM the right way:
Make an observation
Ask a question
Make a Hypothesis
Conduct experiments
Report your results
Draw Conclusions
